Hello!

I am a PhD Candidate in Computer Science and Engineering at the University of Texas at Arlington, advised by Prof. Shirin Nilizadeh. My research focus lies at the intersection of Security, Privacy, and Machine Learning with the aim of building robust and secure AI systems. I've been exploring vulnerabilities in modern AI systems, particularly in large language models and vision-language models, earning recognition for my work on adversarial attacks and phishing detection in commercial LLMs. I am passionate about developing defenses against adversarial attacks and data poisoning, with recent work spanning from text summarization models to medical imaging applications. I am a recipient of several awards including the Distinguished Paper Award at IEEE S&P 2024.

Before starting my PhD, I worked in the industry at Hyundai Mobis as a Graduate Engineer, focusing on automotive software development. I received my Master's and Bachelor's degrees in Computer Science and Electronics & Communication respectively. My transition to security and privacy research was driven by the increasing importance of building trustworthy AI systems that can withstand adversarial threats in real-world applications.

I am always open to new collaborations and discussions about AI security research. Feel free to reach out if you want to chat about research or if you're looking for mentorship in security and privacy!

Transparency or Deception: Subtle Manipulations to Trick Automated Privacy Policy Analyzer Tools
Submitted to USENIX 2025
Developed a novel framework to expose vulnerabilities in automated privacy policy analysis systems using multi-level adversarial text perturbations, with ongoing research into robust defense strategies.
Poojitha Thota, Tanusree Tithy, Shirin Nilizadeh, Faysal Shezan.

Attacks against Abstractive Text Summarization Models through Lead Bias and Influence Functions
EMNLP 2024
Investigated vulnerabilities in text summarization models through adversarial perturbations and data poisoning attacks. Identified and exploited inherent biases using influence functions to effectively poison datasets.
Poojitha Thota, Shirin Nilizadeh.
PAPER CODE
From Chatbots to Phishbots?: Phishing Scam Generation in Commercial Large Language Models
IEEE S&P 2024 - Distinguished Paper Award
Explored capabilities of commercial LLMs to produce evasive phishing attacks by crafting malicious prompts. Designed a classifier for early detection of malicious prompts achieving 98% accuracy using fine-tuned RoBERTa.
Roy, Sayak Saha, Poojitha Thota, Krishna Vamsi Naragam, Shirin Nilizadeh.
PAPER MODEL CODE
Demonstration of an Adversarial Attack Against a Multimodal Vision Language Model for Pathology Imaging
IEEE ISBI 2024
Evaluated and exposed vulnerabilities in pre-trained Vision-Language models, notably PLIP, for pathology imaging. Successfully implemented Projected Gradient Descent attacks with 100% success rate.
Poojitha Thota, Jai Prakash Veerla, Partha Sai Guttikonda, Mohammad S. Nasr, Shirin Nilizadeh, Jacob M. Luber.
